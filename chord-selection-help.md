# Chord Selection Guide

## Overview
The chord selection interface allows you to choose which chord types you want to practice in your piano training sessions. The chords are organized into families (Triads, 7th Chords, and 9th Chords) to make selection easier and more intuitive.

## How to Use the Chord Selection Interface

### Chord Families
Chords are grouped into three main families:
- **Triads**: Basic three-note chords (Major, Minor, Diminished, Augmented, Sus2, Sus4)
- **7th Chords**: Four-note chords that include a 7th interval (Major 7, Dominant 7, Minor 7, Diminished 7, Half-Diminished 7, m(maj7))
- **9th Chords**: Five-note chords that include both 7th and 9th intervals (Dominant 9, Major 9, Minor 9, m(maj9))

### Selecting Chord Types
1. Click on a chord family header (e.g., "Triads") to expand or collapse that section
2. Click on individual chord type buttons to toggle them on/off
3. Use the "Select All" checkbox in each section header to quickly select or deselect all chord types in that family

### Active Chord Summary
At the bottom of the chord selection panel, you'll see a summary of all currently selected chord types. This gives you a quick overview of what you're practicing. You can also click the "Clear All" button to deselect all chord types at once.

### Chord Type Descriptions

#### Triads
- **Major**: Root, Major 3rd, Perfect 5th (C-E-G)
- **Minor**: Root, Minor 3rd, Perfect 5th (C-Eb-G)
- **Diminished**: Root, Minor 3rd, Diminished 5th (C-Eb-Gb)
- **Augmented**: Root, Major 3rd, Augmented 5th (C-E-G#)
- **Sus2**: Root, Major 2nd, Perfect 5th (C-D-G)
- **Sus4**: Root, Perfect 4th, Perfect 5th (C-F-G)

#### 7th Chords
- **Major 7**: Root, Major 3rd, Perfect 5th, Major 7th (C-E-G-B)
- **Dominant 7**: Root, Major 3rd, Perfect 5th, Minor 7th (C-E-G-Bb)
- **Minor 7**: Root, Minor 3rd, Perfect 5th, Minor 7th (C-Eb-G-Bb)
- **Diminished 7**: Root, Minor 3rd, Diminished 5th, Diminished 7th (C-Eb-Gb-Bbb)
- **Half-Diminished 7**: Root, Minor 3rd, Diminished 5th, Minor 7th (C-Eb-Gb-Bb)
- **m(maj7)**: Root, Minor 3rd, Perfect 5th, Major 7th (C-Eb-G-B)

#### 9th Chords
- **Dominant 9**: Root, Major 3rd, Perfect 5th, Minor 7th, Major 9th (C-E-G-Bb-D)
- **Major 9**: Root, Major 3rd, Perfect 5th, Major 7th, Major 9th (C-E-G-B-D)
- **Minor 9**: Root, Minor 3rd, Perfect 5th, Minor 7th, Major 9th (C-Eb-G-Bb-D)
- **m(maj9)**: Root, Minor 3rd, Perfect 5th, Major 7th, Major 9th (C-Eb-G-B-D)

## Tips
- Start with just a few chord types and gradually add more as you become comfortable
- Practice related chord types together (e.g., Major and Minor triads)
- Use the "Invert chords" toggle to practice chord inversions
